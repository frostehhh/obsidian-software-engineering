# Issues
## "War is peace", - Orwell
- Agile processes center work around so-called self-organizing teams.
	- autonomous in a sense that it has control over what it's doing
	- Agile teams manage themselves and their own work. Not project managers.
	- Agile center around people. If you're not treating people well, you're not working in an Agile way.
- There's only the products in an agile systems. There's no projects for a project manager to manage
## "Ignorance is Strength"
- There is a notion that you are strong by not knowing everything(?)
- Agile is about maximizing value to people who use our software/product
- Important part of agile is making small additions to your product and asking for fast feedback from user community
- Emphasis on self-organizing teams. It's okay to have multiple variations of agile processes within a company. If everyone is doing the same thing, that's not agile. Rigidty and agile don't mix. For example, it's okay with have 1 day sprints, 1 weeks sprints, 2 week sprints.
## "Freedom is Slavery"
- Incorrect notion that freedom means chaos and then slavery.
- With independent teams, there can be autonomy of work.
- In a real agile organization, teams have huge amounts of autonomy. They get to decide who goes to their team. They do their own hiring.
- In Lexagle, tech team has limited freedom with what we can do. We are forced to take tasks from product, and design. 
## "Scrum is Agile"
- Truth is, scrum gives rigidity, but agile doesn't work with that.
- Agile is about flexibility. Scrum doesn't have that. 


# What is agile?
- People have to be first. Self-organization is a sign of that. Trust people to get the job done. If you don't trust teams to do the work, you'll slow down.
- Working software is all that matters. High quality and it works well. Release more frequently.
- We learn as we work.
- If your code is shit, it makes it hard to make changes, and it slows you down. As a result, this isn't agile.
