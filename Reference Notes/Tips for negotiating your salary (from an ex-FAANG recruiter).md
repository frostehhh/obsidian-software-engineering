- When should you give out your salary expectations

- Format for negotiation
# Responding to Salary Expectations Question
- Ideally, do not give this answer yet.
- I want to know more about your compensation structure so that I can evaluate my expected on that.
- Ask if the recruiter can provide a minimum range for the salary.
  You may comment that salary budget for the same role on paper means varying ranges across different companies. Can I request a range so that I may propose a more fitting salary expectation based on your budget?
- As much as possible, be neutral.
- "Hey, I haven't been able to give this much thought since I've been busy preparing for interviews. Do you mind if we push this back to a later part of the process?"

| Question                                     | Good                 | Bad                              |
| -------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Recruiter: "The minimum salary range is...", | That's a good start! | That's way lower than I expected |
# Do's and don'ts
- Do pleasantly push back on salary expectation question and a reasonable explanation why
# Format for Negotiation
After the compensation structure is informed to you, you can discuss the negotiation over email. This makes it easier for the recruiter to prepare for a proper response especially if the discussion may be brought to a meeting.
