# Importance of Behavioral Interviews
- There will be a clearer distinction of a candidate's level in these interviews since they will be talking about their personal experiences.
- On the contrary, system design interviews for candidates of different levels may look similar, and not convincing enough to indicate the candidate's level

# Evaluating Candidates in Behavioral Interviews
- They look at specific aspects
	- initiative
	- perseverance
	- conflict resolution
	- leadership
	- communication skills
	- handling ambiguity

# Understanding Interviewing Signals
- Good to journal your work - note talking points for interview
- High impact -> good stories

# Presenting Your Experience Effectively
- Simple context is okay. Your interviewer is most likely very highly experienced
- Action is most important

# Importance of Authenticity in Interviews

# Common Candidate Mistakes and Red Flags
- Top 2 important questions to plan
	- Tell me about yourself
	- Tell me about your favorite project

## How to answer the "Tell me about yourself?" question
- 3 part framework
	- Initial introduction of self + a twist
		- Hi, I'm Luis, I'm 28 and software engineer for 4 years and I'm interested in...
			- Can be "nitty gritty details"
	- preferably high-value impact in your recent roles relevant to the position applied
		- Can be bad to be a history lesson
	- forward-looking statements
		- Can be related to what you can do in the company, role
		- Can be what growth you want or growth itself
		- Can be specific technologies or skills to grow
