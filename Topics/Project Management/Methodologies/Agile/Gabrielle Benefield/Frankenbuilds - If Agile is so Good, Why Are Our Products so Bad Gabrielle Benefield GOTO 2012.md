Are agile, scrum, and kanban really solving our problems?

Agile just pushed more through the system faster

On time and on budget doesn't matter if it's the wrong thing for the wrong people.

We deliver a lot of new features. Could we have made the same amount of success with less and more refined features.
- It is a possible mistake that business thinks that more features is equal to more money. Though, this doesn't necessarily apply to all.
- The more features that you send out, the worse it actually gets.
- The typical customer expects to use the primary features of a product with a convenient experience when they think of using it again. They usually don't think like, "Oh, I wonder what new feature we have?"

# Build outcomes over outputs

Velocity and all these estimating stuff are just getting in the way of development work and getting value out faster.

Outcomes are the goals that you want to get to.

Options are the potential ways you get there.

Never ask the users what they want. Never ask the developers what the users think they want.

Find out what users need.

# Set Target Outcomes
- poke yoke of product development
- make it easy to build the right thing, and hard to build the wrong thing.
- Target outcomes need to be clear and measurable
- How to measure pieces that matter? We're not measuring to get the most precise points. We do sprint/poker planning to share knowledge.
- Target outcomes set a direction, and there are many ways to achieve it. 
- when you plan based on outputs, you commit to a certain future - **BAD**
- Stop pretending you can see the future
- Create options, and keep them open


- Emphasis on changes that are small enough for us to get feedback from users. Build minimum viable product as much as possible. 
- Measure as you go. A lot of people think it's just for product. 
	- Better to share data with the development team. When product sets targets, give those numbers to the developers.
		- What changed in the company
		- What feedback we got
		- etc.
